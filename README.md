# 🌡️ Heat Perception Research Series

Welcome to MeiziYou's Heat Perception Research.

We study how the public perceives and talks about heat on social media. Using large language models, we extract and interpret these voices at scale — across cities, seasons, and climate contexts — to transform scattered public expressions into structured signals that serve climate adaptation and public health.

Our goal is to bridge the gap between what people *feel* about heat and what the data *shows*, and to explore the value of this perception data for climate change and health research.

---

## 📚 Paper Series

### Paper 1 · Spatiotemporal Patterns & Signal Validation

**Global multi-city heat perception: Seasonal dynamics and climate-health signals**

The first study maps heat perception across multiple global cities, revealing its spatiotemporal patterns and validating its association with climate and health indicators.

🔗 **Repository:** [Heat-Perception-1](https://github.com/MeiziYou-Research/Heat-Perception-1)

---

### Paper 2 · Attention & Motion Framework

**Heat Perception Attention & Motion (HPAM) Framework**

Building on the first study, we propose a deep learning architecture that combines Attention and Motion mechanisms to model the dynamics of public heat perception, enabling climate-health intelligence applications.

🔗 **Repository:** [Heat-Perception-2](https://github.com/MeiziYou-Research/Heat-Perception-2)

---

## 🔬 Methodology Overview

**Data** → Social media posts about heat from multiple cities worldwide

**Analysis** → Large language models for semantic parsing and perception extraction

**Validation** → Cross-referencing with meteorological and epidemiological records

**Modeling** → Deep learning frameworks for temporal pattern recognition and prediction
